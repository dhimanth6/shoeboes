WE GOT TIRED OF WEARING
OTHER PEOPLE'S STORIES.

It didn’t start in a pristine design studio. It started with scuffed midsoles on concrete, jumping fences, and the heat of the city tarmac in Hyderabad.


For years, sneaker culture meant importing culture from overseas. We were wearing the history of Chicago basketball courts and London underground scenes, while ignoring the streets right outside our windows.


GullyLabs is our correction.


We take the raw materials of our streets—the dust of the maidan, the violent rain of the monsoon, the midnight neon of the tea stalls—and stitch them into silhouettes that speak our language.
